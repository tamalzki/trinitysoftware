JMRosel Trucking Services

Fleet operations · Dispatch · Expenses · Payroll

LaravelMySQLLive system

Context

A trucking operator running regional trips needed one place to see what moved, what it cost, and what drivers should be paid — without reconciling spreadsheets every week.

The problem

Dispatch lived in one workflow, fuel and expenses in another, and payroll in a third. Trip-level detail did not tie cleanly to pay rules, so closing a pay period meant manual matching and constant back-and-forth. That slowed operations and increased error risk.

Business model

Revenue is trip- and route-driven: each run has revenue potential, variable cost (fuel, tolls, maintenance), and driver compensation rules. The business only stays profitable when every trip’s cost and pay can be explained from the same operational truth — not from reconciled estimates.

How we fixed it

We delivered a single Laravel application with three tightly linked modules:

  • Dispatch — trips, vehicles, and status in one board so operations sees live movement.
  • Expenses — costs captured against trips and cost centers so margin is visible per run.
  • Payroll — pay rules and outputs grounded in the same trip and expense data, reducing reconciliation to exception handling instead of default work.

One database, one audit trail, role-based access — so leadership can trust the numbers and the team spends less time fixing them.

DivineJM Foods

National food distribution · 30+ branches · Production & finance

LaravelMulti-branchBOM / ERP

Context

A food business distributing nationwide with many branches must know, at any moment: what raw stock exists, what is being produced, what finished goods sit where, what is on consignment, and whether the books reflect reality.

The problem

Branch inventory, production, consignment, and finance were at risk of drifting apart: production might consume materials that weren’t decremented correctly; branch stock might not match what sales could promise; consignment and ledger entries could lag behind operations. That breaks trust in margin and availability.

Business model

The model combines manufacturing (raw → finished via recipes / BOM), multi-location inventory (what each branch can sell or transfer), and consignment & sales with a full financial trail. Profitability depends on accurate COGS, waste visibility, and branch-level performance — all of which require one operational source of truth.

How we fixed it

We built a Laravel + MySQL platform that connects the full chain:

  • Master data — raw materials, finished products, areas, and customers structured for scale.
  • Production — production mixes and BOM-style logic with automatic raw-material deductions when product is produced.
  • Branch inventory — visibility per branch for what can ship or transfer.
  • Consignment & sales with ledger-grade treatment so finance and operations read the same story.
  • Reporting for operational and financial leadership without exporting to fragile spreadsheets.

Angie — Inventory & Finance

Two branches · Inventory · Sales · Daily cashflow

LaravelCashflowLedger

Context

A multi-branch food-style operation needed enterprise-grade discipline with a smaller footprint: two locations, but the same need for tight inventory, production alignment, and daily visibility into cash.

The problem

Owners could not afford to wait for month-end to know if the business was liquid and healthy. Inventory and sales data had to connect to cash in and out every day; otherwise corrective action came too late.

Business model

The business earns through product movement (sales, consignment) and manages tight working capital: money tied in stock, receivables, and payables must be visible alongside daily operating cash. The “model” is sustainable only when cashflow, inventory, and books stay aligned.

How we fixed it

We implemented the same structural strengths as a larger distribution build — inventory sync, production and material logic, consignment and sales ledger — and added daily cashflow dashboards so leadership sees cost, profit, and liquidity as the day unfolds, not only after books close.

Citipower Electronic Supply

Purchase orders · Inventory · Sales · Branch-ready

LaravelPO syncRules engine

Context

An electronics supply business lives or dies on accurate stock and fast order-to-cash. Purchase orders, receiving, and sales must update the same inventory truth.

The problem

When POs, receiving, and sales were updated manually or in separate silos, counts and commitments drifted: sales could promise stock that receiving hadn’t reflected, or purchasing could reorder off stale numbers.

Business model

Margin comes from turning inventory and serving trade customers reliably. The model depends on transaction-driven stock: every PO receipt, adjustment, and sale updates the same ledger of quantity and value so pricing and fulfillment stay honest.

How we fixed it

We wired inventory, sales, and purchase orders in one Laravel system so a PO receipt updates stock and downstream behavior can follow rules by transaction type. The deployment runs one branch today; the data model and roles are structured to scale to additional branches without rebuilding core flows.

Ronnie Budiongan Aircon Services

Serialised equipment · Field service · Installments

LaravelService jobsCollections

Context

HVAC businesses sell units and parts that must be tracked individually, and they earn repeatedly through install, repair, and maintenance. Many customers pay on installment plans, so collections must be as visible as inventory.

The problem

Generic retail inventory is not enough: the business needs serial-level traceability for equipment and parts, job-based service revenue, and installment schedules that don’t get lost in a generic “accounts” column.

Business model

Revenue mixes product margin and recurring service, with cash flow supported (or stressed) by planned customer payments. The model requires tying every unit to a serial, every job to a customer, and every installment to a clear schedule.

How we fixed it

We implemented serial-level inventory, service job modules for core revenue activities, and an installment plan view so monthly collections are visible and missed payments surface early — reducing revenue leakage and disputes.

AAG Maritime Management Services

Marketing site · Maritime · Lead generation

WebSEO-readyLead forms

Context

AAG provides shipping, seaport, and maritime services to corporate and institutional clients. The website’s job is to establish credibility quickly and convert interest into qualified inquiries — not just look good.

The problem

Maritime buyers compare vendors on trust, clarity, and responsiveness. A weak or confusing site loses deals before a conversation starts: services must be scannable, differentiation clear, and contact friction low.

Business model

The commercial model is B2B project and retainer-style work: win trust online, route leads to sales, then deliver complex logistics services offline. The site is a top-of-funnel asset — it must support brand positioning, service packaging, and measurable lead capture.

How we fixed it

We built a focused marketing presence with clear service narratives, structured sections for maritime offerings, and inquiry paths that match how prospects evaluate vendors. The implementation leaves room to grow (case studies, partner logos, richer content) without restructuring the core site — so marketing can evolve without a rebuild.
